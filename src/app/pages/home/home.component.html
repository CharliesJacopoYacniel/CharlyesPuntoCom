About

Charlies Yacniel is a Software Designer / Developer for a local company in which he has worked on various projects for three years, and is also a resident of the city of Tegucigalpa. In 2018, he graduated from the National Autonomous University of Honduras, taking with him the title of Systems Engineer and the passion for what he does today.

Charlies works building both the creative and the development parts of the projects, supporting some and raising others from analysis. The 2020 COVID-19 quarantine transformed Charlies' career from typical job to remote job, building two projects along the way.

Charlies was previously a carpenter in his father's workshop, a business that has now become a family business. He also had some business going from the PC repair business, selling Pizzas to the silk screen service.

As of today, if Charlies isn't throwing lines of code, solving some equation, pushing pixels, putting together the Rubik's cube, or strengthening his abs, you can find him studying online platforms, building new foundations, reinforcing knowledge, and gaining new skills.

Let's go! Go and write Charlies at holamundo@charlyes.com

More about me

Systems engineer by profession, Programmer by hobby, Software Developer by occupation, camping fan, memero, dyslexic, coffee lover and nomad par excellence.

My friends describe me as an easygoing person, a good listener, good with pun and a great pizza cook.

I like it ...

I like to solve problems come on! Come give me a problem and I will give you at least a solution, if I can't find it I will at least recommend you to restart.

"If 1 + 1 never gave you '11' you failed as a programmer"
Anonymous

I like the design and development of systems, I like that my users feel comfortable using my applications and that they want to see my next project. I like to draw the flow lines of my systems and see the expressions of my clients when they say that the solution is what they need.

"Never trust a programmer in a suit"
Anonymous

I like to curse in front of the computer when something goes well and also when it doesn't. The taste for the visual concept began when I saw the attractive designs that my colleagues captured and received good acceptance from others, then I looked at mine and felt how my colleagues point to my designs asking: "Do you still design with tables?" indirectly they forged my character. All of this together gave a push to focus on what I really like.

"It was missing more, when you had not started"
Orlando Tejada

I like the technology industry, I do the "work" I do because I enjoy it and I firmly believe that there is nothing more rewarding than that.

I thank my family and the people I know, because in one way or another they influenced who I am now.

Linus Torvalds
